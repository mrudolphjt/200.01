You are here to begin.

You have walked into a training that is going to ask something a little unusual of you. Before you learn what to teach, it is going to ask you to notice how you show up.

That is not a stylistic choice. It is the heart of LEAD WELL.

The framework you are preparing to teach is not a curriculum to deliver. It is a practice to live.

The way you carry it is what people will believe is possible.

You are not here to master something today. You are here to begin.

How you show up matters.

Part of showing up as a LEAD WELL trainer includes approaching interactions with a sense of gratitude—for the time, trust, and lived experience others are willing to share.

⏱

Time

Participants are choosing to spend attention, energy, and time in this space.

🤝

Trust

People may share experiences that require care, humility, and respect.

🧭

Lived Experience

The work becomes meaningful when real stories are welcomed and honored.

Gratitude keeps the trainer from treating facilitation as performance. It shifts the stance from “I am here to deliver content” to “I am here to serve the learning, the people, and the practice.”

Who this is for

This orientation is designed for well-being specialists, leadership development colleagues, and embedded leaders responsible for culture and staff experience.

Prior exposure to the LEAD WELL Framework or to the evidence-based well-being intervention known as The Practice is helpful, but not required.

1
Well-being specialists
Colleagues supporting staff experience, resilience, and well-being.
2
Leadership development colleagues
Professionals helping leaders translate concepts into practical behaviors.
3
Embedded leaders
Leaders responsible for culture, trust, team experience, and day-to-day practice.

What you will leave with

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to describe the LEAD WELL framework and the role of a trainer, name three behaviors of a LEAD WELL trainer, and decide whether this program is right for you at this point in your work.
